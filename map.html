Chair of Hetepheres

Date: 2550 BCE
Material: cedar wood adorned with gold leaf, copper, and faience inlays
Location: Saqqara, Egypt
Designer: Unknown

First chair to be recorded, still standing.

Throne

Date: 300 - 200 BCE
Material: marble
Location: Athens, Greece
Designer: Unknown

One of the only remaining Greek marble chairs, features scenes from greek mythology.

Tyldal Chair

Date: c. 1100
Material: carved wood
Location: Tyldal, Norway
Designer: Unknown

Replica of a Viking Dining chair. Designed in 1100, replica carved in 1920.

Chinese Folding Chair

Date: c. 1300
Material: lacquer treated bamboo
Location: China
Designer: Yuan Dynasty, designer unknown

Oldest example of the folding chair. Was a household and court necessity.

Armchair

Date: c. 1700
Material: carved roots
Location: China
Designer: Quin Dynasty, designer unknown

Made from carved tree roots. Often used by scholars and intellectuals.

Revolving Windsor Chair with Writing Arm

Date: 1775
Material: Wood
Location: New England, US
Designer: Unknown

First ever swivel chair. Thomas Jefferson signed the Declaration of Independence from this chair

Campeche Chair

Date: c. 1815
Material: Mahogany, embossed leather
Location: Yucatan, Mexico
Designer: Unknown

Popular style of Mexican chairs, which were commonly imported to America.

Chair

Date: 1850-1950
Material: wood, leather, and brass tacks
Location: Democratic Republic of Congo
Designer: Chokwe artist

From Chokwe people of Africa. Partially influenced by the European chairs colonizers brought.

Thonet 18

Date: 1876
Material: beech wood
Location: Vienna, Austria
Designer: Michael Thonet

Result of the bent wood industry. First mass-produced and afforable style of chair.

Armchair

Date: 1900
Material: wood and upholstery
Location: Paris, France
Designer: Otto Wagner

Designed for the Paris 1900 Exhibition. Displayed new ideas for furniture design.

Wassily Chair

Date: 1925
Material: steel and leather
Location: Dessau, Germany
Designer: Marcel Breuer

Made of tubular steel, inspired by bicycle frames and club armchairs.

Monobloc Chair

Date: 1946
Material: plastic
Location: Winnipeg, Canada
Designer: Douglas C. Simpson, revised by Henry Massonnet in 1972

The plastic chair we all know and love. So affordable, it can be seen across the world.

Eames Chair

Date: 1956
Material: plywood and leather
Location: Zeeland, Michigan
Designer: Charles and Ray Eames

Hallmark of modern interior design. Cited as one of the most counterfeited pieces of furniture in history.

Ball Chair

Date: 1963
Material: fiberglass and fabric
Location: Veikkola, Finland
Designer: Eero Aarnio

Often associated with the space age, or villiany in movies.

IKEA Inflatable chair (ROLIG)

Date: 1999
Material: inflatable plastic and fabric
Location: Älmhult, Sweden
Designer: Jan Dranger

Chair that required a hairdryer to "inflate". Costly to produce and would not stay inflated.
